AI-Powered Streetlights

Smart, Adaptive, and Energy-Efficient Urban Lighting

About the Innovation

We have developed AI-powered streetlights that adjust the brightness of their LEDs dynamically based on human and/or vehicular detection. Each streetlight includes a camera and an AI model that is built in to the product and can also detect and track movement while also filtering out irrelevant triggers like birds or other small animals/distractions. The lights also have the ability to tilt, this is to follow individuals or vehicles more efficently and accurately. This ensures visibility while aslo minimizing our energy waste. In large crowds, they will remain stationary but can also increase brightness strategically to reduce any unnecessary light pollution.

Key Features

  • AI-Powered Precision: Only detects relevant objects to minimize any false activations.
  • Dynamic Axis Movement: Lights will tilt to have a seamless experience for the user.
  • Adaptive Brightness: Adjusts light intensity based on traffic and pedestrians.
  • Light Pollution Reduction: Dims when no movement is detected. This reduces power consumption and light pollution
  • Smart Urban Integration: It is fully autonomous and helps to enhance smart city initiatives.
  • Surveillance: The footage is not recorded and is only used to process in real time.

Business Model and Scalability

We operate as a smart infrastructure startup that is focused on AI-powered lighting systems to help improve light pollution and also current lighting solutions. The revenue model we have made includes contracts with local governments, smart city programs, and urban planning agencies as well.

Key Operations:

  • Research and Development: To further optimize the AI model in the future
  • Manufacturing: Producing streetlights with embedded AI systems and high quality imaging.
  • Deployment and Maintenance: Ensuring long-term reliability and regular maintenance
  • Sales and Investments: Partnering with governments to retrieve investment.

Growth Strategy

  • Year 1: Initial deployment of product, further optimization and alsosecuring funding.
  • Years 2-3: Attempt to expand to multiple cities, software updates, and grow strategic partnerships.
  • Years 4-5: International growth and stay in line with emerging market trends.
